COVID-19: A Visual Essay

This visual essay will narrate the timeline of the COVID-19 pandemic around the world, and the effect the vaccines had on the scale of the outbreak.

Firstly, we will examine how the pandemic spread across different countries and continents from its onset. Next, we will see how the G7 countries managed the pandemic. We will also investigate whether there is a correlation between the relative wealth of a country and the spread of the pandemic. Finally, we will look at the impact of vaccinations on the spread of cases and deaths.

How did the pandemic spread across the world from the onset?

Cases initially started appearing around the 5th of January 2020 in China, Germany and Finland.

On February 1st, the total cases was 12K and appearing in Western Europe, North America, and Southeast Asia.

On April 1st, the total cases was 894K with Europe growing the most rapidly.

On September 1st, the total cases was around 26M with Asia now growing the fastest.

By February 1st 2021, total cases globally exceeded 100M with Europe and North America growing the fastest.

On August 1st, total cases are about to exceed 200M with Asia being the fastest growing.

On February 1st 2022, total cases globally is around 379M.

On March 7th 2023, total cases is around 759M.

How successfully did the G7 countries manage the pandemic? Is there a relationship between the relative “wealth” (e.g. GDP) of a population and the spread of the pandemic?

Hover over the legend text/circle to highlight that line.

The Show Total Deaths / 1M People chart shows that only Japan had lower deaths per 1M people than the global average. Meanwhile, the rest of the G7 had some of the highest values despite being the wealthiest countries.

The Show Total Cases / 1M People chart shows a similar story expect even Japan is higher that the global average.

Japan managed to keep it's stringency lower and more stable than it's counterparts while having lower cases/deaths as shown in Show Stringency Index chart.

The Total Vac. Doses / 100 People chart shows the G7 countries were very successful in administering vaccine doses compared to the global average with Japan beating the rest of it's peers.

It's clear from the above charts that the relative wealth of a country and the spread of the pandemic are correlated.

What effect did vaccinations have on the spread of cases/deaths?

Hover over the legend text/circle to highlight that line.

The above chart shows vaccinations had a positive effect on the number of cases globally. As the number of vaccinations rises, the number of deaths slows.

Conclusion

In this visual essay, we have seen how the pandemic spread across the globe, starting in China, Germany and Finland, then across Western Europe, North America, and Southeast Asia, then grow rapidly across the world.

We also compared how the G7 countries handled the pandemic compared to the global average and found a correlation between the relative wealth of a country and the spread.

We also concluded that the vaccinations has a large effect in reducing the spread of deaths.
